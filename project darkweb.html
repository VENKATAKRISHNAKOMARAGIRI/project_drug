ABOUT THE BLOODY DRUG

Drug injections are a method of delivering medication directly into the body using a syringe and needle. This method allows the drug to enter the bloodstream quickly, making it useful for emergencies, pain relief, or treatments that require fast or precise dosing. Injections can be given in various ways, including intravenous (into a vein), intramuscular (into a muscle), or subcutaneous (under the skin). Despite their effectiveness, drug injections come with several disadvantages. They can cause pain, swelling, or bruising at the injection site. There's also a risk of infection if proper hygiene is not maintained.

Repeated injections may lead to tissue damage or scarring. In some cases, individuals may develop allergic reactions or experience side effects due to the rapid absorption of the drug. For people who inject drugs recreationally, sharing needles greatly increases the risk of transmitting serious diseases such as HIV and hepatitis. Additionally, injections often require trained medical personnel, making them less convenient than pills or topical medications in non-clinical settings.
